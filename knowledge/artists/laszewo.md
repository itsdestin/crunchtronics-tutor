# Łaszewo

## TL;DR
Łaszewo (the Ł is a Polish slashed-L, pronounced roughly "wah-shev-oh") is a Santa Barbara trio — producers Matthew Ehrlich and Justin De La Fuente plus singer-songwriter Keeva Bouley — making indie-pop-leaning electronic music that lives in the EDM ecosystem more by association than by sound. With 5 tracks in Destin's pull, they're mid-leaderboard and the *softest* artist in the library by some margin. Genre-adjacency note: the closest existing cheat sheet is `../genres/melodic-dubstep.md` because both share the "pretty vocal lead + electronic production" frame, but Łaszewo is downtempo / chill (mean 92 BPM) — not dubstep at any half-time interpretation. They're an indie-pop / electropop band first.

## Cheat block
- **BPM range:** 80.0-105.0 (mean 92.5). Downtempo / chill territory — completely outside the 124-150 dance-music center of Destin's library. This is the slow-evening listen on the playlist.
- **Common keys:** 7A — D minor (1 track); 11B — A major (1 track). Small enriched sample, no strong tonal lean.
- **Signature sound design:**
  - Keeva's vocal *is* the lead — sung indie-pop topline, dry-ish, gently doubled, more "singer-songwriter" than "EDM vocalist."
  - Production is sparse: 808 + clap drum loop, soft pad, electric piano or a clean synth lead, no aggressive bass or drop.
  - Dynamics are conversational rather than loud-quiet-loud — the song doesn't "build to a drop"; it builds to a vocal hook and rests there.
  - Effects are subtle: tape saturation, light reverb, occasional vinyl crackle for texture. No heavy sidechain, no big risers.
- **Sample tracks from your library:**

## Read this yourself
Łaszewo is the artist on the leaderboard most likely to teach Destin a *songwriting* lesson rather than a sound-design lesson. None of the existing genre cheat sheets in `knowledge/genres/` is a good fit — they're not bass-house, not tech-house, not dubstep, not melodic-dubstep. They're closer to a contemporary indie-pop band like Glass Animals or boy pablo, just produced by EDM-adjacent producers. The melodic-dubstep cheat sheet at `../genres/melodic-dubstep.md` is the closest existing reference *only* in the sense that both Łaszewo and melodic dubstep prioritize melodic / emotional content over rhythmic aggression.

The honest read: when Destin's curriculum gets to "writing topline melodies and chord progressions," Łaszewo is the artist on the leaderboard that most rewards close listening for that skill. Their tracks are unusually slow for the rest of the library and unusually song-shaped (verse-chorus-bridge rather than intro-build-drop-break-drop). Studying a Łaszewo track teaches arrangement decisions that don't appear in tech-house or dubstep — when to leave the drum kit out, when to bring it in, how a vocal hook earns its place at the front of the mix.

The Polish "Ł" is the band's signature visual — preserve it whenever writing the band name. The slug uses ASCII `laszewo` because filesystem and URL conventions don't play well with diacritics.
