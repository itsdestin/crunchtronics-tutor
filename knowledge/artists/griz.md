# GRiZ

## TL;DR
GRiZ (Grant Kwiecinski) is a Detroit electronic artist who fuses funk, soul, jazz, and bass music into a signature live-instrument-driven sound — he plays the saxophone parts himself. With **14 tracks** in Destin's pull (under all-credits counting), he's the **#1 artist on the leaderboard**. He does not cleanly fit any of the four existing genre cheat sheets (`bass-house.md`, `dubstep.md`, `melodic-dubstep.md`, `tech-house.md`); bass-house is the closest because his drops sit in that BPM/energy zone, but the funk-bass and live-saxophone leads are unique to him. 4 of 14 are co-credits — he sits at a similar collaboration-hub position to Subtronics on the heavy-bass side.

## Cheat block
- **BPM range:** 60.7-170.0 (mean 125.8). The wide spread reflects how genre-fluid his catalog is — jazz cuts down at 60-90, electro cuts at 100-110, bass-house and dubstep at 130-150, with a 170 outlier likely a half-time-felt 85 BPM groove or a remix.
- **Common keys:** 8B — C major (2 tracks); 10B — D major (1 track); 3B — Db major (1 track). Major-key lean — funk/soul roots show through.
- **Signature sound design:**
  - Live saxophone as the lead — recorded clean, then often pitch-shifted, chopped, or stuttered for the EDM context. Recreating this in Ableton means actually sampling sax, not synthesizing it.
  - Bass character is funk-rooted: a slap-bass or Moog-style mono lead with movement (filter, vibrato, pitch slides) rather than a static tech-house sub.
  - Drum kit blends programmed 808s with live snare/hat samples for organic feel.
  - Vocal samples (often soul-style) chopped and pitched, layered as a melodic instrument rather than a focal hook.

## Read this yourself
GRiZ is the artist on Destin's leaderboard who most rewards an instrument-first approach to learning. Where Disco Lines, jigitz, John Summit, and Łaszewo are all built on synths and samples, GRiZ records actual saxophone, plays actual bass, and arranges those takes around an electronic rhythm section. That makes him a bridge between Destin's junior-high piano background and the dance-music curriculum: he proves that the path forward isn't "abandon real instruments and go all-synth" but "learn to integrate live takes into Ableton."

The closest existing cheat sheet is `../genres/bass-house.md` because GRiZ's drops occupy that frequency / energy / BPM space. But in the cheat sheet's own framing, GRiZ's bass is *not* a Pendulum-style snarl — it's a 70s funk slap-bass routed through a fuzz pedal and a stereo widener. Honest read: the catalog ranges across funk-dubstep, jazz-house, soul-leaning bass music, and traditional electro; treat each track as a separate study. The "Mercy" YouTube breakdown above is the single best entry point for Destin: GRiZ walks through arrangement choices, not just sound design, and it's the rare artist breakdown that's both authoritative (the producer himself) and beginner-friendly.
