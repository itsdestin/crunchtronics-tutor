# Ian Asher

## TL;DR
Ian Asher is a 24-year-old SoCal producer / DJ who broke through with viral TikTok mashups (his Jain "Makeba" remix has 10B+ TikTok plays) and now sits in the disco-house / mashup-edit pocket — vocal-led, four-on-the-floor, club-energy-first. With 5 tracks in Destin's pull he's mid-leaderboard. Genre-adjacency note: the closest existing cheat sheet is `../genres/tech-house.md` because the rhythmic skeleton is identical, but his catalog leans more disco-house and edit-style than pure tech-house.

## Cheat block
- **BPM range:** 131.8-170.2 (mean 148.8). Higher cluster than typical tech-house (124-128), reflecting the festival-bigroom / hard-house edges where mashup-DJ producers live.
- **Common keys:** 11A — F# minor (2 tracks); 11B — A major (1 track); 8B — C major (1 track). F# minor is a default key on the Camelot wheel for mashing — easy to mix into both major and minor neighbors.
- **Signature sound design:**
  - Vocal-as-hook is non-negotiable — chopped, pitched, sometimes reversed, often a recognizable indie / pop sample (his catalog leans on flips of Black Out Days, Glass Animals, etc.).
  - Drums are punchy, bigroom-leaning rather than tech-house-tight: layered kick (sub + click), wide clap, white-noise riser into every drop.
  - Bass is melodic and present — usually a saw lead doubling the bass an octave up, more "festival-anthem" than "club-roller."
  - Heavy use of risers, downlifters, and impacts at every transition — DJ-set-friendly arrangement.

## Read this yourself
The single most useful thing about Ian Asher for a beginning producer is the *workflow* he embodies: he built a career out of fast turnaround edits and mashups posted to TikTok. That means his tracks are often *not* full original productions — they're a chopped vocal acapella riding on top of a programmed bigroom / disco-house instrumental. For Destin learning Ableton, this is a much lower-friction entry point than a from-scratch original: pick an acapella, pick a four-on-the-floor template, time-stretch / pitch the vocal to fit the project key, and arrange. The tech-house cheat sheet at `../genres/tech-house.md` covers the underlying drum/bass skeleton; what's specific to Ian Asher is the vocal-flip-first arrangement.

The honest read on the catalog: it's not a single coherent sonic identity yet — the man is 24 and still figuring out what his sound is. The 11A / F# minor concentration in the data above is a tell that he's working in a Camelot-friendly key for DJ-set use rather than picking keys for emotional reasons. That's also a useful learning point: when in doubt, compose in F# minor or A minor and your tracks will mix smoothly into other people's sets.
