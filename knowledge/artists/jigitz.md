# jigitz

## TL;DR
jigitz is one of the master-spec-named taste anchors for Destin's library and sits at 7 tracks. He's an Albuquerque-native, NYC-based producer whose debut EP *all my exes live in brooklyn* (R&R, 2025) blends breakbeat, future garage, ambient textures, and house rhythms — genre-fluid indie dance rather than a single bucket. Genre-adjacency note: bass-house is the closest existing cheat sheet (`../genres/bass-house.md`) by BPM and energy, but the catalog is closer to indie-dance / future-garage with breakbeat percussion than to a Pendulum-style bass-house.

## Cheat block
- **BPM range:** 130.0-160.0 (mean 145.0). The 130-140 cluster is house-leaning; the 145-160 pocket is where the breakbeat / future-garage tracks sit.
- **Common keys:** 11B — A major (2 tracks); 1B — B major (1 track). Major-key lean is unusual for this energy band — most bass-music sits in minor.
- **Signature sound design:**
  - Breakbeat / 2-step shuffled drum loops alongside four-on-the-floor — not a strict 4/4 metronome.
  - Bass is round and synthy, not snarled — closer to a UK garage reese than a US bass-house growl.
  - Pads and atmospheric textures (reverb tails, vinyl crackle, processed field-recording-ish layers) sit much higher in the mix than in any other artist on Destin's leaderboard.
  - Vocals are melancholic and present-but-not-loud — sung indie pop topline rather than chopped sample, often delivered by featured vocalists.
- **Sample tracks from your library:**

  - make believe
  - free myself
  - tell you straight
  - pressure

## Read this yourself
jigitz is named in the master spec as one of Destin's confirmed taste anchors, which makes this page load-bearing for the curriculum. The interesting tension: he's anchored as bass-related in the spec, but a careful listen shows he's actually closer to indie dance / future garage. The 7 tracks in the pull skew melancholic-major and breakbeat-leaning; the rhythmic spine is *not* a kick-on-every-beat tech-house template.

For curriculum purposes, the takeaway is: when Destin says "I want to make tracks like jigitz," the lesson plan should not start with `../genres/bass-house.md` straight up. It should start with the four-on-the-floor / sidechain skeleton from `../genres/tech-house.md`, then layer in breakbeat percussion (a separate module worth its own future cheat sheet), then the future-garage atmosphere (pads, reverb tails). Sound design is more about *space* than *aggression* — the opposite of bass-house's defining stance.

There's also a useful career-shape lesson in the Billboard piece: jigitz made his early tracks at a 9-to-5 office job, posted them to SoundCloud, refused to put them on Instagram for fear of being seen, and only quit his day job once "Tell You Straight" was charting. That's a Destin-relatable creative-confidence story worth keeping in mind.
