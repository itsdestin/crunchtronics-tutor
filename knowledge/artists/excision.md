# Excision

## TL;DR
Excision (Jeff Abel) is a heavy-dubstep / tearout originator and the founder of Lost Lands festival. He appears 4 times in Destin's library — **always as a collaborator**, never as a sole-credit primary. He's a peer to Subtronics on the heavy-bass leaderboard with a longer career arc, and his publicly-stated multi-DAW workflow ("depends on the song, cubase, logic, ableton, FL") makes him a less Ableton-specific learning target than Subtronics.

## Cheat block
- **BPM range:** 90.0-157.1 (mean ~129 across 3 enriched; 1 unenriched). The 90 outlier is "Don't Look Down" felt half-time over 180; the 140 cluster is core dubstep; 157 is the "Zombie" collab high-end. Modal interpretation: 140 BPM is the genre default; he half-times to 70-90 felt for emotional builds.
- **Common keys:** 9A — E minor (1), 11A — F# minor (1), 7A — A minor (1), 1 unenriched. Minor-leaning — the heavy-dubstep default. Both E minor and F# minor are festival-set keys; A minor is the "Don't Look Down" half-time melodic feel.
- **Signature sound design:**
  - Bass: aggressive mid-forward growls and tearout textures with dense wavetable modulation; the spiritual ancestor of Subtronics' "robot noises" sound. Excision's catalog runs longer and his early work is the bridge from Skrillex-era dubstep to modern tearout.
  - Drums: layered kicks (sub + click transient), heavily processed snares with reverb tails, sparse hat patterns; consistent with the broader heavy-dubstep idiom.
  - Hybrid moves: at the lower BPM cluster (90-138 felt) he leans into orchestral / cinematic builds (the "Zombie" collab is the clearest example), then drops into half-time tearout. This mirrors ILLENIUM's cinematic-pre-drop pattern but with heavier drop sound design.
  - Multi-DAW workflow per his own answer to a fan question — does not commit to one DAW per song; uses Cubase, Logic, Ableton, and FL Studio depending on the project.
- **Sample tracks from your library:**
  - Zombie (feat. Valerie Broussard) — with ILLENIUM and Wooli
  - Air (feat. Julia Michaels) — with Gryffin
  - Don't Look Down (Hold On) — with Wooli and Codeko
  - Bonebreaker — with Sullivan King and Kai Wachi *(unenriched: `miss:reccobeats` — bpm/key TBD)*

## Read this yourself
Excision is a *historical* study target as much as a contemporary one. He's been releasing heavy-bass music for over fifteen years; his catalog is the trace of how festival dubstep evolved from 2010-era Skrillex sound design into the modern tearout / riddim hybrid that Subtronics now leads. For a beginner producer, that means his early tracks are surprisingly approachable — the sound design is heavy but the song structures are simpler than current Subtronics-era tearout.

The multi-DAW workflow is a curriculum-relevant data point. Unlike Subtronics (Ableton-first, public about it) or Wooli (Ableton-first, taught himself in it), Excision uses whatever DAW fits the song — including FL Studio, Cubase, and Logic alongside Ableton. That makes him a worse direct workflow-study target for a Live 12 Lite beginner than Subtronics, but a better case study in *DAW-agnostic sound design*: the bass growls and drum-layering techniques are independent of the DAW.

The collaborator-only credit pattern in Destin's library (4 of 4) reinforces this: Excision has stayed relevant into the 2020s by featuring on younger artists' tracks — Wooli, Gryffin, ILLENIUM, Sullivan King, Kai Wachi. That pattern itself is a career-shape lesson worth noting. The "Zombie" collab is the highest-leverage study target because it sits at the four-way intersection of Destin's heavy-bass leaderboard: ILLENIUM's cinematic builds, Excision's heavy drop, Wooli's melodic-dubstep treatment, and Valerie Broussard's vocal hook all in one track.

For first-pass listening, the Wooli "Don't Look Down" collab is the most accessible — it's a half-time felt 90 BPM track with a clear melodic structure before the drop, and the drop itself is heavy but not maximally tearout. That's a softer entry point into the Excision idiom than "Zombie" or anything from his solo catalog.
