# ILLENIUM

## TL;DR
ILLENIUM (Nick Miller) is a melodic-dubstep / future-bass crossover headliner who appears 4 times in Destin's library — **always as a collaborator or remix author**, never as a sole-credit primary. He's a peer-tier artist to Wooli on the melodic-dubstep curriculum path, with a documented Ableton-Live workflow and a supersaw-wall sound that maps directly to `../genres/melodic-dubstep.md`.

## Cheat block
- **BPM range:** 136.0-157.1 (mean 143.8 across 4 enriched). The 144 BPM cluster is on the genre-default melodic-dubstep anchor; 136 is the future-bass-leaning lower end (the Halsey "Without Me" remix); 157 is the high-end on the Wooli/Excision "Zombie" collab.
- **Common keys:** 10B — D major (2 tracks), 9A — E minor (1 track), 2B — F# major (1 track). Major-leaning — consistent with the future-bass / cinematic-uplift end of melodic dubstep, distinct from Wooli's minor-leaning supersaw drops.
- **Signature sound design:**
  - Drops are built on layered supersaw walls — multiple oscillators with careful detuning, each layer targeting a specific frequency band to avoid mud. The genre-defining technique covered in `../genres/melodic-dubstep.md`.
  - Cinematic build moments — orchestral risers, vocal-chops, pitched percussion — placed at the pre-drop to lift emotional weight before the drop hits.
  - Vocal hooks are a centerpiece — featured-vocalist topline material processed with formant-shifting and long-tail reverb (consistent with Wooli's approach).
  - Future-bass chord-stab vocabulary at the lower BPM end (136-138 cluster); melodic-dubstep half-time spine at 140-145.
- **Sample tracks from your library:**
  - Zombie (feat. Valerie Broussard) — with Excision and Wooli
  - Without Me — ILLENIUM Remix (Halsey original)
  - Feel Good (with Daya) — with Gryffin
  - Good Things Fall Apart — with Jon Bellion

## Read this yourself
ILLENIUM is the highest-leverage *secondary* study target on the melodic-dubstep curriculum path — secondary because Wooli is the named master-spec anchor. But ILLENIUM has two advantages that make him a strong reinforcement: he uses Ableton Live as his main DAW (verified via Equipboard), so workflow lessons translate directly; and his sound is more openly templated than Wooli's, with multiple third-party Ableton templates available for study.

The hybrid future-bass / melodic-dubstep blend is the load-bearing pattern. Future bass contributes the chord-progression vocabulary and chopped-vocal motifs (achievable in Ableton Live 12 Lite with stock Operator + Simpler); melodic dubstep contributes the half-time spine and supersaw drop walls (achievable with Operator stacks, upgraded once Vital or Serum is in the toolchain). Practice target: build a track that lives at 140 BPM half-time, opens with a future-bass chord-stab pre-chorus, drops into a supersaw wall on the half-time spine. That's the ILLENIUM template, and it's the right second melodic-dubstep project after a Wooli-style first attempt.

The collaborator-only credit pattern in Destin's library (4 of 4) is informative: ILLENIUM is a hub artist who features other vocalists and producers heavily. The Halsey "Without Me" remix is the most accessible study target on this list because the original is a well-known pop song and the remix transformation is clear — kick-and-vocal builder → 140 half-time supersaw drop. That contrast is the genre formula in one track.
