# BUNT.

## TL;DR
BUNT. is a Berlin folktronica / melodic-house duo built on acoustic-guitar loops, banjo, hand-claps, foot-stomps and earnest "campfire" vocal hooks dropped into four-on-the-floor dance grooves. They aren't a clean fit for any existing cheat sheet in `knowledge/genres/` — folktronica / melodic-house is their primary inhabit — but melodic-dubstep is the closest lens because both share the "pretty-on-top, rhythmic-engine-underneath" structure. With **10 tracks** in the pull (under all-credits counting; the +1 over the v1.0 count is the Zedd × VALORANT × Foxes "Clarity" BUNT. Remix, where they're the remix author), BUNT. is one of the top-three artists in Destin's library and the main "warm / organic" voice in an otherwise synthetic catalog.

## Cheat block
- **BPM range:** 128.1-175.2 (mean 142.9). The cluster sits in two pockets: a 128-130 melodic-house pocket and a 140-150 hybrid pocket where they double-time the kick into faster drops.
- **Common keys:** 8A — A minor (3 tracks); 2B — F# major (2 tracks); 10A — B minor (1 track). Heavy minor lean, with A minor as the home key.
- **Signature sound design:**
  - Acoustic guitar / banjo as the primary lead — finger-picked, often capoed, sometimes layered with a mandolin or ukulele for the high-frequency sparkle.
  - Foot-stomp + hand-clap percussion treated as a sample group: gated, transient-shaped, sat next to the kick and clap to keep the "live in a barn" feel even on synthetic drums.
  - Vocal treatment is dry and close — pitch-corrected only lightly, often double-tracked an octave up rather than processed with heavy effects. The vocal *is* the lead.
  - Bass is supportive, not aggressive — usually a sub-sine following the root note, with sidechain to make space for the kick. The drops trade dubstep's growl for a wider, more musical low end.
- **Sample tracks from your library:**

## Read this yourself
BUNT. is the artist on the leaderboard who *least* sounds like the rest of Destin's library. Where Disco Lines, jigitz, and John Summit are all working in synthetic, club-first palettes, BUNT. starts from acoustic instruments and builds outward. That makes them a useful study counterpoint: the rhythmic spine is still four-on-the-floor with sidechain (see `../genres/tech-house.md` for the underlying pattern), but the timbral palette is borrowed from indie folk. Learning to recreate a BUNT. drop teaches one specific Ableton skill set — sample-flipping acoustic loops, transient-shaping foot stomps, and pitching a guitar to fit the project key — that's separate from the synth-heavy work the rest of the curriculum will emphasize.

The band's public-facing material on production technique is thin (no widely-circulated Production Music Live breakdowns or Splice "Sounds of" pack as of 2026-04-28). The honest read is: study the records by ear, drag a few of their stems into Ableton's Sampler, and reverse-engineer the mix. The melodic-dubstep cheat sheet at `../genres/melodic-dubstep.md` is the closest existing reference for "pretty melodic content riding on top of an EDM rhythmic engine," even though BUNT.'s drops are softer than Seven Lions'.
