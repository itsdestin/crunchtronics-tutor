# nimino

## TL;DR
nimino (Milo Evans) is a UK-based producer who blends house, UK garage, hip-hop, and lo-fi into a melodic chill-bass hybrid. Hand-picked by Destin during the brainstorm. Genre-adjacency note: the closest existing cheat sheet (`../genres/melodic-dubstep.md`) is listed as the prereq because of mood and emotional register, but nimino sits at 122-127 BPM and uses garage / 2-step rhythms rather than 140 half-time — the catalog is closer to a UK chill-bass cousin of `../genres/tech-house.md` than to dubstep proper.

## Cheat block
- **BPM range:** 122.0-127.0 (mean 124.7). Tight cluster — this is fundamentally a 124-127 BPM artist, the same pocket as house and tech-house, not the 140 dubstep grid.
- **Common keys:** 4B — Ab major (1 track); 10A — B minor (1 track); 3A — Bb minor (1 track). Major-leaning across the small sample but the minor-key tracks are the more atmospheric ones.
- **Signature sound design:**
  - Drums: shuffled garage / 2-step patterns with off-grid hat placement — not strict four-on-the-floor.
  - Bass: round, melodic, often a single sub note with movement (slides, octave jumps) rather than aggressive growl design.
  - Vocal samples are a centerpiece — pitched, chopped, layered as a melodic instrument; the "I Only Smoke When I Drink" hook is the obvious example.
  - Atmospheric textures (vinyl crackle, room reverb, soft pads) sit prominent in the mix, giving a lo-fi / "world-building" feel.

## Read this yourself
nimino is a useful artist to study because his catalog occupies a slot none of Destin's existing genre cheat sheets cover cleanly: melodic / chill-bass at 122-127 BPM with UK garage rhythms. That makes him a bridging artist between the tech-house pocket (124-128) and the melodic-dubstep emotional palette, but with a totally different drum feel (shuffled / 2-step rather than four-on-the-floor or 140 half-time). For curriculum purposes, he's worth revisiting *after* tech-house and melodic-dubstep are both familiar — at that point his sound becomes legible as a hybrid rather than a mystery.

The strong vocal-chop centerpiece in his work is also a transferable lesson: nimino doesn't write huge hooks, he finds tiny vocal phrases (often featured-vocalist topline material, sometimes royalty-free samples) and treats them as melodic synth parts. Recreating this in Ableton Live 12 Lite is achievable with stock tools — Simpler in Slice mode + manual chopping is the path. No external sampler needed.

Honest read on the resource gap: nimino broke through commercially in 2024 with "I Only Smoke When I Drink," and as of this write-up the official preset / sample-pack ecosystem hasn't caught up. The third-party YouTube track-analysis above is the best entry point; expect a Splice pack or producer interview to land in the next 12-18 months as his profile grows.
