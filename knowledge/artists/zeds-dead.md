# Zeds Dead

## TL;DR
Zeds Dead (DC and Hooks, Toronto) are one of the duos that helped define North American dubstep in the early 2010s. **7 tracks** in Destin's pull (under all-credits counting; the +1 over v1.0 is the Blue Foundation "Eyes on Fire" Zeds Dead Remix), 4 of which come from the same album, *Return to the Spectrum of Intergalactic Happiness*, which means a single album-focused interview unlocks production context for most of the catalog Destin has saved. They're explicitly genre-fluid — the duo's stated approach is "we're never really going for a genre."

## Cheat block
- **BPM range:** 83.4-150.2 (mean 127.0). The wide spread reflects how genre-fluid the catalog is — downtempo / hip-hop crossover at 83-90, mid-tempo at 100-110, dubstep proper at 140-150. The mean ~127 sits in between, which is itself diagnostic: there's no single tempo center.
- **Common keys:** 6A — G minor (1 track); 10A — B minor (1 track); 8A — A minor (1 track). All minor — bass-music default. Spread across the pull rather than dominated by one key.
- **Signature sound design:**
  - Sampling-first approach — the *Spectrum* album is built around what MusicTech describes as "a century of sound" sampled and recontextualized. That's the production identity, not a single synth signature.
  - Bass character varies by track: classic Skrillex-era growls on heavier cuts, sub-only on downtempo cuts, snarled mid-range layered with sub on the dubstep proper.
  - Vocal samples often replace traditional toplines — chopped, pitched, layered as melodic instruments.
  - Drum kits blend programmed dubstep skeletons with live-feeling textures (room reverb, vinyl crackle, tape hiss) — similar in philosophy to GRiZ but with bass-music weight rather than funk-soul instrumentation.
- **Sample tracks from your library:**
  - One Of These Mornings
  - Ecstasy Of Soul — with GRiZ
  - Fall Away
  - Sweet Memories
  - Heartbeat — with Minke
  - Think Of You — with Blunts & Blondes
  - Eyes on Fire — Zeds Dead Remix (Blue Foundation original)

## Read this yourself
The MusicTech *Spectrum* interview is the highest-leverage resource on this page by a wide margin. Four of Destin's six saved Zeds Dead tracks come from that album, and the interview is specifically about how the album was made — the sampling philosophy, the genre-fluid arrangement, the studio decisions. That's the rare case where one external article unlocks production context for a large chunk of the artist's catalog in the user's library.

The strategic takeaway for the curriculum is that Zeds Dead's catalog is *not* a single-genre study target. Treat each track as its own project: "One Of These Mornings" is downtempo / lo-fi, "Heartbeat" is mid-tempo bass, the dubstep cuts are dubstep. The duo's own framing — "Dubstep and all these bass genres are just kind of things we dabble in" — is the right framing for Destin to adopt when he eventually picks a Zeds Dead track to teardown.

For Live 12 Lite specifically: the sampling-first identity translates well. Lite ships with Simpler in Slice mode, which is the right tool for the chopped-vocal / chopped-found-sound approach the *Spectrum* album leans on. The harder element to recreate is the bass sound design across the heavier tracks; that wants Vital or Serum, same as Subtronics. The third-party "Bumpy Teeth" Serum patch is genre-adjacent but represents a Zeds Dead × Subtronics collab specifically, so it's a partial preview of one bass character, not the whole catalog.
