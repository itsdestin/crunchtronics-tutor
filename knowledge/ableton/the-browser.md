# The Browser

## TL;DR
Left-side panel for finding sounds, instruments, devices, and your own files. The Browser is your one-stop shop: every Ableton instrument and effect, every factory pack, every Splice/sample-folder location you've added — all searchable in one place. Drag from the Browser to a track to load it.

## Cheat block
- **Toggle:** **Cmd/Ctrl + Alt + B** opens or closes the panel.
- **Top-left categories:** Sounds, Drums, Instruments, Audio Effects, MIDI Effects, Max for Live (Suite only), Plug-ins, Clips, Samples.
- **Places** (lower section): your own folders. Add Splice or sample-pack folders here — they show up alongside Ableton's stock content.
- **Search box** (top of Browser): live filter as you type. Type "kick" → only kick samples and Drum Rack chains containing the word kick.
- **Hover-preview:** click the headphones icon at the bottom of the Browser → samples preview when you hover them. Tempo-synced to your set.
- **Hot-swap:** when a Simpler / Drum Rack pad is selected, the little arrow icon in the device header puts the Browser in "swap mode" — single-click to load.
- **Collections:** colored tags to mark favorites. Right-click any item → Add to Collection.

## Read this yourself
For the first weeks you'll mostly live in two parts of the Browser: **Drums** (factory drum kits to scaffold a beat) and **Sounds** (Ableton's curated multi-device patches — full instruments with effects pre-wired). Both are clickable enough that you don't have to know anything about synthesis to start writing.

Once you collect Splice packs or your own samples, drop those folders into Places (right-click "Add Folder…" near the bottom of the Browser). Tag what you love with Collections — yellow for kicks you trust, red for vocal chops, blue for one-shot stabs — and your library becomes searchable in your own taxonomy instead of by some packaging-engineer's idea of "Trap Vol. 4 / Kicks Hard / Hard 12.wav".
