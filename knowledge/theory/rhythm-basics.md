# Rhythm basics

## TL;DR
Beats, bars, and time signatures — the pulse and grid that all EDM is built on top of. A "beat" is one tick of the underlying pulse; a "bar" is a recurring group of beats (almost always four in EDM); the "time signature" tells you how many beats are in a bar and how long each one is.

## Cheat block
- **BPM:** beats per minute. The tempo. EDM lives mostly in the 70-180 range.
- **Beat:** one click of the metronome.
- **Bar (measure):** a group of beats. In 4/4, one bar = 4 beats.
- **Time signature:** top number = beats per bar; bottom number = which note value gets one beat (4 = quarter note). EDM is overwhelmingly 4/4.
- **Four-on-the-floor:** kick on every beat (1, 2, 3, 4). House / tech-house / disco backbone.
- **Off-beat hat:** open hi-hat on the "and" between every beat. The classic house "tss tss tss".
- **Subdivisions:** 1/4 (one per beat), 1/8 (two), 1/16 (four), 1/32 (eight). Most EDM hi-hats run at 1/16.
- **Phrase length:** EDM thinks in 16-bar and 32-bar phrases (intros, drops, breakdowns).

## Read this yourself
The single most useful mental shift when you start producing is to stop counting in seconds and start counting in bars. A 128 BPM tech-house track has 128 beats every minute, which means 32 bars every minute, which means a 16-bar intro lasts exactly 30 seconds. Once that math becomes second nature, song structure stops being mysterious.

When you're at the MPK, turn on Ableton's metronome (the metronome icon in the top transport bar) and play along to it. Count "1-and-2-and-3-and-4-and" out loud — the numbers are beats, the "ands" are 1/8 notes between them. Tap a quarter-note kick on pad 1 to start. Once that locks in, add an off-beat hat on the "ands". Congratulations: you have the spine of every house track ever made.
